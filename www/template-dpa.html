GDPR Data Processing Agreement

1. Parties
This Data Processing Agreement (“DPA”) is entered into between:
1.1. The organisation (or organisations) that the user (that uses the Service) represents when using the Service, hereafter referred to as (the “Customer”)
1.2. Relation Desk AB, Swedish company reg. no. 556900-0523, (“Relation Desk”) with address at Klarabergsviadukten 63 2tr, 111 64 Stockholm, Sweden. hereinafter jointly referred to as “Parties” or individually a “Party”.
2. Background
2.1. Relation Desk provides a cloud-based software solution (SAAS) to the Customer which is designed to facilitate and manage social media. The Parties have entered into a separate agreement that specifies the terms of conditions for the Customer’s access to and use of the cloud based software solution (the “Service”).
2.2. Upon performance of the agreement regarding the cloud-based software solution, Relation Desk will be Processing Personal Data on behalf of the Customer in capacity of the Customer’s data processor. The Customer is the data controller of the Processing of Personal Data. For the purpose of ensuring compliance with the Data Protection Rules, the Parties enters this DPA which forms an integral part of the entire agreement between the Parties (the “Agreement”).
3. Definitions
”Data Protection Rules” means Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data and on the free movement of such data (General Data Protection Regulation, or “GDPR”).

”Data Subject” means the identified or identifiable natural person whom the Personal Data relates to.

”Personal Data” means any information, which directly or indirectly relates to a Data Subject and which Relation Desk Processes on behalf of the Customer under this DPA.

”Processing” means any operation or set of operations which is performed on Personal Data, or on sets of Personal Data, whether or not by automated means.

”Sub-Processor” means any third party that Processes Personal Data on behalf of Relation Desk (including, but not limited to, Relation Desk’s partners and subcontractors)

”Supervisory Authority” means the independent public supervisory authority/supervisory authorities, authorised to conduct supervision of the Processing of Personal Data or considered to be a “supervisory authority concerned” in accordance with the Data Protection Rules.

Unless otherwise stated, any other term or concept used in capitalised letters in this DPA (except in some cases as part of a heading) shall have the meaning and conception that is established in the Data Protection Rules and otherwise in the Agreement, unless the circumstances obviously require another interpretation.
4. Responsibility and Instruction
4.1. The Customer is the data controller of all Personal Data Processed by Relation Desk on behalf of the Customer. The Customer undertakes to comply with the Data Protection Rules. The Customer shall regularly inform Relation Desk of measures taken by third parties in relation to the Processing carried out by Relation Desk, hereunder by the Supervisory Authority and by Data Subjects.
4.2. The Personal Data that Relation Desk Processes on behalf of the Customer is specified in Appendix 1 (Data processing instructions). The Customer shall ensure that Relation Desk does not Process any other categories of Personal Data than those specified in Appendix 1 on behalf of the Customer.
4.3. Relation Desk, and anyone working under Relation Desk’s supervision, shall only be Processing Personal Data in accordance with the Customer’s documented instructions and not for any other purposes than the purposes the Customer has hired Relation Desk for under the Agreement. Relation Desk may however analyze and use data on an aggregated level (not containing any Personal Data). The instructions that apply to this DPA are specified in Appendix 1. In addition, the Agreement constitutes the Customer’s instructions. The Customer shall immediately inform Relation Desk of changes that affect Relation Desk’s obligations according to this DPA. Processing may also be performed by Relation Desk or Sub-Processor where required by EU law or applicable member state law, which Relation Desk or Sub-Processor is subject to.
4.4. The Customer shall inform Relation Desk in case anyone else, either alone or jointly with the Customer, is Data Controller(s) of the Personal Data.
5. Security and Confidentiality

The measures taken by Relation Desk to fulfil this obligation are described in Appendix 1. The Customer shall ensure that the measures described in Appendix 1 fulfil the security requirements under the Data Protection Rules for the Personal Data that are Processed by Relation Desk on behalf of the Customer.
5.2. Relation Desk shall document the technical and organisational security measures that are taken to comply with the security requirements under the Data Protection Rules and this DPA. The documentation shall be provided to the Customer upon written request.
5.3. Relation Desk shall assist the Customer in ensuring compliance with the obligations pursuant to Articles 32 to 36 of the GDPR, taking into account the nature of Processing and the information available to Relation Desk.
5.4. Relation Desk shall notify the Customer without undue delay after becoming aware of a personal data breach pursuant to Article 33 of the GDPR. Notification of any personal data breach will be delivered to the Customer’s contact details in this DPA or, at Relation Desk’s discretion, by direct communication (for example, by phone call or an in-person meeting).

6. Disclosure of Personal Data and Information
6.1. Relation Desk, or anyone working under Relation Desk’s supervision, shall not disclose Personal Data, or information about the Processing of Personal Data, without the Customer’s express instruction or as laid down in this DPA, unless required by the Data Protection Rules. Relation Desk shall forward any request to the Customer from the Data Subject, the Supervisory Authority or any other third party, who is requesting receipt of information regarding Personal Data that Relation Desk Processes on behalf of the Customer.
6.2. Relation Desk shall, by technical and organisational measures which are appropriate taking into account the nature of the Processing, assist the Customer, insofar as this is possible, for the fulfilment of the Customer’s obligation to respond to requests from the Data Subject, when the Data Subject exercises its rights in accordance with the Data Protection Rules.
6.3. Relation Desk shall inform the Customer of any contacts from the Supervisory Authority that concern the Processing of Personal Data on behalf of the Customer. Relation Desk is not entitled to represent the Customer or act on the Customer’s behalf towards the Supervisory Authority.
7. Sub-Processors
7.1. If Relation Desk engages a Sub-Processor, Relation Desk shall enter into a data processing agreement with the Sub-Processor where the same data protection obligations as set out in this DPA are imposed on the Sub-Processor. If the Sub- Processor fails to fulfil its data protection obligations, Relation Desk shall remain fully liable towards the Customer for the performance of the Sub- Processor’s data protection obligations. The Customer hereby generally authorises Relation Desk to engage any other third parties within and outside the EU/EEA to Process Personal Data as a Sub-Processor.
7.2. Relation Desk is in particular responsible for ensuring the compliance of Articles 28.2 and 28.4 of the GDPR when engaging Sub-Processors, and to ensure that Sub-Processors provide sufficient guarantees to implement appropriate technical and organisational measures, in such a manner that the Processing meets the requirements of the GDPR.
7.3. Upon the Customer’s request, Relation Desk shall provide such specified information regarding Processing by Sub-Processors, which the Customer reasonably may request according to the Data Protection Rules.
7.4. Relation Desk shall inform the Customer of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of Sub-Processors. The information shall describe the name of the Sub-Processor and the place where the Processing takes place. The Customer is also entitled to, upon written request, receive information about which type of Processing the Sub-Processor will conduct on Relation Desk’s behalf.
7.5. The Customer is entitled to make a written objection to the changing of a Sub-Processor within 30 days from when Relation Desk has notified the Customer of such change. If the Customer makes such objection, and Relation Desk does not accept to replace the Sub-Processor or refrain from using a Sub- Processor, the Customer have the right to terminate the part/parts of the Agreement which the Processing by the Sub-Processor concerns within 30 calendar days from the day when Relation Desk informed the Customer about the choice to not replace the Sub-Processor or refraining from using the Sub- Processor. The notice period for such termination shall be 90 days.
7.6. If the Customer terminates the Agreement in accordance with section 7.5 above, wholly or in part, Relation Desk shall, provided that the Customer’s termination of the Agreement is based on a justified reason for the objection, repay any payments that have already been made by the Customer to Relation Desk that refers to the period after the termination period and the terminated part of the Agreement. If the Customer’s objection is justified Relation Desk is not permitted to use the new Sub-Processor for Processing on behalf of the Customer during the Customer’s notice period. If the Customer does not have a justified reason for the objection, the Customer’s objection will be regarded as an early termination of the Agreement, wholly or in part, without cause (meaning, inter alia, that the Customer shall pay to Relation Desk any and all remaining fees and charges as agreed in the Agreement). In that case, Relation Desk shall be entitled to compensation from the Customer for all Relation Desk’s costs and expenditures that result from not being able to use the Sub-Processor in question. In this clause, “justified reason” means circumstances on the Sub-Processor’s side that are likely to significantly affect or in fact significantly affect the protection of the Data Subject’s personal integrity. Such a circumstance may, for example, be that the Sub- Processor does not comply with the requirements imposed on data processors according to the Data Protection Rules.
8. Audits etc.
Within reasonable time upon the Customer’s request, Relation Desk shall make available all information necessary to demonstrate Relation Desk’s compliance with its obligations following from the Data Protection Rules, including as part of the audits or inspections carried out by the Customer or an independent auditor mandated by the Customer and accepted by Relation Desk.
9. Transfers of Personal Data outside the EU/EEA and Data Portability
9.1. Relation Desk and/or Sub-Processors may transfer Personal Data to a location outside of the EU/EEA provided that Relation Desk and/or Sub-Processor ensures that such transfer complies with applicable Data Protection Rules.
9.2. Relation Desk shall assist the Customer in fulfilling potential duties to enable data portability regarding Personal Data, which Relation Desk Processes under this DPA.
10. Compensation
Relation Desk is entitled to compensation for all work, costs and expenditures stemming from following the Customer’s data processing instructions, which are not clearly documented in the Agreement and/or when resulting in work that goes beyond functions and the level of security following from the services that Relation Desk normally provides to its customers and/or when the data processing instructions changes during the term of this DPA. Relation Desk shall also have the right to compensation for performance of its obligations as described in sections 5.4, 6.2, 8, 9.2, 12.2 and 13.1. Compensation shall be paid in accordance with Relation Desk’s from time to time applicable price list.
11. Liability
11.1. If Relation Desk, anyone working under Relation Desk’s supervision, or Sub- Processors, Processes Personal Data in violation of this DPA or contrary to lawful instructions of the Customer, Relation Desk shall pay damages to the Customer for the direct damage suffered by the Customer due to the incorrect Processing, in accordance with the limitation of liability that follows in the Agreement. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in the Agreement, Relation Desk’s liability under this section 11.1 shall, for each 12 month period with start January 1 every year, be limited to an amount corresponding to 100 % of what the Customer has paid to the Relation Desk during the preceding calendar year. Relation Desk’s liability for each damage or claim shall be limited to an amount corresponding to 100 % of what the Customer has paid to Relation Desk during the calendar year preceding the damage or claim. Furthermore, Relation Desk shall not be liable for any indirect or consequential damages.
11.2. During the term of this DPA and thereafter, the Customer shall indemnify and hold Relation Desk harmless from any direct or indirect damage, e.g. claims from the Data Subject and other controllers of the Personal Data than the Customer, when Relation Desk has suffered such damage due to unclear, inadequate or unpermitted instructions from the Customer, or otherwise, depending on the circumstances on the Customer’s side.
11.3. Relation Desk’s obligation to pay damages, laid down in section 11.1 above, only applies, provided that i) the Customer without undue delay informs Relation Desk in writing of any claims against the Customer; and ii) the Customer allows Relation Desk to control the defence of the claim and make independent decisions regarding conciliation.
11.4. This clause 11 shall remain valid also after this DPA is terminated, regardless the cause.
12. Term and Termination
12.1. This DPA enters into force on May 25, 2018 if duly signed by both Parties before or on May 25, 2018. If this DPA is agreen on by both Parties after May 25, 2018 it shall enter into force on the day when both Parties have signed it. This DPA remains in force as long as Relation Desk Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Customer. This DPA replaces all previous agreements between the Parties that regulates the processing of personal data, but this DPA does not replace the parts of previous agreements between the Parties that regulates the purchase of and payment for access to the Service.
12.2. Upon termination of the Agreement or this DPA (depending on which is first terminated), Relation Desk shall in accordance with the Customer’s instructions delete the Personal Data that the Customer has transferred to Relation Desk and delete any existing copies, where appropriate, and unless storage of the Personal Data is required by EU law or applicable member state law.
13.Changes and additions
13.1. If the Data Protection Rules are changed during the term of this DPA, or if the Supervisory Authority issues guidelines, decisions or regulations concerning the application of the Data Protection Rules that result in this DPA no longer meeting the requirements for a DPA, the Parties shall make the necessary changes to this DPA, in order to meet such new or additional requirements. Such changes shall enter into force no later than thirty (30) days after a Party sends a notice of change to the other Party or otherwise no later than prescribed by the Data Protection Rules, guidelines, decisions or regulations of the Supervisory Authority.
13.2. Other changes and additions to this DPA, in order to be binding, shall be made in writing and signed by both Parties.
14.Miscellaneous
14.1. This DPA supersedes and replaces all prior DPAs between the Parties and supersedes any deviating provisions of the Agreement concerning the subject matter of this DPA, regardless if otherwise stated in the Agreement.
14.2. This DPA shall be interpreted in accordance with Swedish law. Swedish law applies in all aspects to Relation Desk’s Processing of Personal Data under this DPA. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this DPA shall be settled in accordance with the dispute resolution provision in the main agreement.
Appendix 1 - Data processing instructions
1. Definitions
In these data processing instructions, all capitalized words shall have the same meaning as defined in the Data Processing Agreement between the parties, unless otherwise is expressly stated.
2. Data processing instructions
Purposes
Relation Desk shall Process Personal Data for the purpose of performing the Service, which is a cloud service, to the Customer in accordance with the Agreement, including all support, maintenance and additional services related to the Service.

Categories of Personal Data and Data Subjects
- The Customer’s employees
Relation Desk shall Process account information related to the Customer’s employees which are using the Service. This includes, inter alia, name, email address, password, id- number on Facebook account from which the employee accesses the Customer’s Facebook page, other information that is needed to deliver the full functionality of the Service, and information about the employee’s activity within the Service (such as which social media posts the employee has responded to and log data regarding time for log in and log out of the Service).

- Individuals who communicate with the Customer in the social media platforms included in the Service
Relation Desk shall Process Personal Data about individuals who communicate with the Customer in the social media platforms included in the Service under the Agreement. The Personal Data Processed by Relation Desk is Personal Data related to the interaction between individuals who communicate with the Customer in the social media platforms included in the Service and said social media platforms. Relation Desk shall Process the Personal Data which may be collected from the social media platform from time to time. The Personal Data consists of, inter alia, name, link to publicly available photo, ID-number to user account in the social media service, the message/post the individual has written and information about the time when the message was posted.

- No special categories of Personal Data shall be Processed
Relation Desk shall not Process any special categories of Personal Data on behalf of the Customer, such as health information, information about religion etc. The Customer is fully liable for ensuring that no other categories of Personal Data than those mentioned above are Processed in the Service.

Processing operations
Relation Desk shall only Process Personal Data to provide the Service and any additional services to the Customer in accordance with the Agreement and/or the Customer’s instructions. The Processing consists of collecting, compiling and storing of information of social media posts made by individuals on the Customer’s social media platforms included in the Service under the Agreement as well as processing of information about the Customer’s employees which are using the Service for the purpose of providing user accounts in the Service and the functionality in the Service. The Processing also consists of necessary technical analyses of Personal Data Processed within the Service for support and maintenance purposes.

Furthermore, the Processing may also include transfer of Personal Data to or from a third party as instructed by the Customer. Such transfer of Personal Data is carried out automatically by integrations to the Service from/to third parties which the Customer has decided to integrate with the Service. The Customer is fully liable to ensure that appropriate security measures have been taken for such transfers.

Location of Processing operations
The Processing shall be carried out within the EU/EEA or outside the EU/EEA if transfer outside the EU/EEA is in accordance with the Data Protection Rules.

Relation Desk shall, upon the Customer’s request, provide the Customer with a specified list of the locations of Procession Operations.

Relation Desk shall keep records of the geographical locations of the IT systems used for Processing Personal Data on behalf of the Customer, including each location of Sub- Processors’ IT systems used for Processing Personal Data on behalf of the Customer. At written request of the Customer and without undue delay, Relation Desk shall make such records available.
3. Information security measures (technical and organisational)
Access control
- Physical Access Control
Relation Desk shall ensure that only such personnel, who needs direct access to Personal Data in order to fulfil Relation Desk’s obligations under the DPA including these instructions, will have access to Personal Data.

- Transfer of Personal Data access control
All transfer of Personal Data shall go through a cryptographic protocol that provide communications security. Access shall be limited to such persons who need the Personal Data in order to fulfil their tasks.

- System access control
Access to systems requires entry with a personal digital key and/or username and password through a cryptographic protocol that provide communications security.
Access shall be limited to such persons who need the Personal Data in order to fulfil their tasks. Digital key and/or User ID and password shall be personal and shall not be transferred or lent to another person. There shall be processes for granting and revoking access.

- Personal Data access control
Access to Personal Data has to be explicitly given through personal non transferable accounts or digital key through a cryptographic protocol that provide communications security.
Access shall be limited to such persons who need the Personal Data in order to fulfil their tasks. Digital key and/or User ID and password shall be personal and shall not be transferred or lent to another person. There shall be processes for granting and revoking access.

Back-up
Backups containing Personal Data are routinely and automatically erased no later than 6 months after the backup is saved and is only accessed by key personel at Relation Desk.

Firewalls and separation of environments
Relation Desk shall isolate development and production systems. Backups are isolated in data-silos outside of application. Firewalls protect data-silos from outside connections and are only accessible by transfers through cryptographic network protocol that protects the communication.

Authorization and permissions
The Customer can have one or several team members who are allowed to grant other individuals or groups of individuals access to the Customer’s Personal Data. This can be done through the system itself or by telling Relation Desk’s staff to do it for them.

Encryption of data communication
Encrypted communication shall be used for all transfers of Personal Data from Relation Desk to the Customer. The exception to this rule is if information that contains personal data is sent to from Relation Desk to the Customer via Email. Emails are not encrypted.

Deletion
Personal Data shall be deleted upon the Customer’s request and in accordance with the Customer’s instructions. The Customer shall in its request specify whether all Personal Data shall be deleted or if the deletion shall only concern part of the Personal Data Processed. If only part of the Personal Data shall be deleted, Relation Desk may ask the Customer for more precise instructions until enough information is gathered to deduce exactly what Personal Data is referred to in the Customer’s request. Unless the Customer has instructed Relation Desk to do otherwise, Personal Data shall be deleted within 90 days from the Customer’s request (provided that Relation Desk has gotten required information).

Development systems
Personal Data shall not be used in development systems. However, if the Personal Data in the backup data has been completely anonymized and thereby lost is status as Personal Data, backup data may be used in development systems.

Other
Relation Desk shall ensure that such personnel, who needs direct access to Personal Data in order to fulfil Relation Desk’s obligations under the DPA including these instructions, receives training and information on how they are allowed to Process the Personal Data. Relation Desk shall have procedures for entering into confidentiality agreements with Relation Desk’s suppliers providing repair and service of equipment, which is used to store Personal Data
